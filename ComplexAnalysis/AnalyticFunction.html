Holomorphic

  • Partial derivative: $\dfrac{\partial}{\partial z} = \dfrac{1}{2}\left(\dfrac{\partial}{\partial x} - i\dfrac{\partial}{\partial y}\right), \dfrac{\partial}{\partial \bar{z}} = \dfrac{1}{2}\left(\dfrac{\partial}{\partial x} + i\dfrac{\partial}{\partial y}\right)$.
  • $\dfrac{\partial \bar{f}}{\partial z} = \overline{\left(\dfrac{\partial f}{\partial {\bar {z}}}\right)},\dfrac{\partial \bar{f}}{\partial \bar{z}} = \overline{\left(\dfrac{\partial f}{\partial {z}}\right)}$.
  • Derivative: $f^{\prime}(z_0)=\lim\limits _{z\to z_{0}}\dfrac{f(z)-f(z_0)}{z-z_0}=\dfrac{\partial u}{\partial x}+i\dfrac{\partial v}{\partial x}=\dfrac{\partial v}{\partial y}-i\dfrac{\partial u}{\partial y}$.
  • Holomorphic on $z_0$ if $f$ is derivable on $U(z_0)$, on a region $\Omega$ if $f$ is derivable everywhere in $\Omega$ and is entire if $f$ is derivative on $\mathbb{C}$.
  • Cauchy-Riemann equation: $f(z) \in \text{Hol}(\Omega) \Leftrightarrow \begin{cases}u,v \text{ is differentiable on } \Omega \\ \dfrac{\partial u}{\partial x}=\dfrac{\partial v}{\partial y},\dfrac{\partial v}{\partial x}=-\dfrac{\partial u}{\partial y}\end{cases} \Rightarrow \dfrac{\partial f}{\partial\bar{z}}=0$.
  • Harmonic: $\Delta u(x,y)=\Delta v(x,y)=0,\text{conjugate } \dfrac{\partial u}{\partial x}=\dfrac{\partial v}{\partial y},\dfrac{\partial v}{\partial x}=-\dfrac{\partial u}{\partial y}$
  • $\begin{cases}f(z) \in \text{Hol}(\Omega) \\ u,v \in C^2(\Omega)\end{cases} \Rightarrow \begin{cases}\Delta u=\Delta v=0 \\ f^{\prime}(z) \in \text{Hol}(\Omega)\end{cases}$.

Integration along curves

Given a smooth curve $\gamma$ in $\mathbb{C}$ parametrized by $z:[a, b] \to \mathbb{C}$, and $f \in C^0 (\gamma)$
  • $\displaystyle{\int_{\gamma}f(z)\mathrm{d}z:=\int_a^b f(z(t))z^{\prime}(t)\mathrm{d}t}, \displaystyle{\int_{\gamma^-}f(z) \mathrm{d}z=-\int_{\gamma}f(z)\mathrm{d}z}$
  • $\displaystyle{\mathrm{length}(\gamma)=\int_a^b |z^{\prime}(t)| \mathrm{d}t}, \displaystyle{\left| \int_{\gamma}f(z)\mathrm{d}z\right| \le \sup_{z \in \gamma}|f(z)|\cdot \mathrm{length}(\gamma)}$.
  • $F^{\prime}(z)=f(z) \text{ in } \Omega \Rightarrow \displaystyle{\int_{\gamma}f(z)\mathrm{d}z =F(z(b))-F(z(a))}$.
  • If $\{f_n(z) \in C^0\} \rightrightarrows f(z)$ on $\gamma$, then $f(z) \in C^0, \displaystyle{\lim\limits_{n \to \infty}\int_{\gamma}f_n(z)\mathrm{d}z=\int_{\gamma}f(z)\mathrm{d}z}$.

Cauchy's Theorem

$\Omega$ is a bounded region with a smooth boundary
  • $f(z) \in C^1(\bar{\Omega}), \displaystyle{\int_{\partial \Omega}f(z)\mathrm{d}z}= \displaystyle{\iint\limits_{\Omega}\dfrac{\partial f}{\partial \bar{z}}2i \mathrm{d}x\mathrm{d}y=-\iint\limits_{\Omega}\dfrac{\partial f}{\partial \bar{z}}\mathrm{d}z\land \mathrm{d}\bar{z}}$.
  • Lemma: $D \in \mathbb{C} \text{ is a triangle region, } f(z) \in \text{Hol}(\bar{D}) \Rightarrow \displaystyle{\int_{\partial D}f(z)\mathrm{d}z=0}$.
  • $f(z) \in C^0(\bar{\Omega})\cap \text{Hol}(\Omega) \Rightarrow \displaystyle{\int_{\partial \Omega}f(z)\mathrm{d}z=0}$
  • $f(z) \in C^0(\bar{\Omega})\cap \text{Hol}(\Omega) , z_0 \in \Omega, z \in D(z_0,r) \subset \Omega$.
  • $\Rightarrow f(z)=\displaystyle{\dfrac{1}{2 \pi i}\int_{\partial \Omega}\dfrac{f(\omega) \mathrm{d}\omega}{(\omega-z)}=\sum\limits_{n=0}^{+\infty}\dfrac{1}{2\pi i}\int_{\partial \Omega}\dfrac{f(\omega) \mathrm{d}\omega}{(\omega-z_0)^{n+1}} (z-z_0)^n}$
  • $\Rightarrow \displaystyle{ f(z_0)=\dfrac{1}{2 \pi} \int_{0}^{2 \pi}f(z_0 + re^{i \theta}) \mathrm{d}\theta=\dfrac{1}{\pi r^2}\iint_{D(z_0,r)}f(z)\mathrm{d}S}$
  • Cauchy: $f(z) \in \text{Hol}(\Omega),z_0 \in \Omega \Rightarrow |f^{(n)}(z_0)| \le \dfrac{n!\sup\limits_{z \in \Omega}\{|f(z)|\}}{(dist(z_0,\partial \Omega))^n}$
  • Liouville: $f(z) \in \text{Hol}(\mathbb{C}) \text{ is bounded} \Rightarrow f(z) \equiv C$.
  • Weierstrass: $f(z) \in \text{Hol}(\mathbb{C}) \ne C,\overline{f(\mathbb{C})}\supset \mathbb{C}$.

Analytic Function

  • Prop. $f(z) \in \text{Hol}(\Omega) \Rightarrow f(z) \in C^{\omega}(\Omega) \Rightarrow u,v \in C^{\omega}(\Omega)$.
  • Morera: $\Omega$ is a region, $f(z) \in C^0(\Omega)$, $f(z) \in C^{\omega}(\Omega) \Leftrightarrow \forall$ bounded region $D$ with smooth boundary, $\bar{D} \subset \Omega, \displaystyle{\int_{\partial D}f(z)\mathrm{d}z}=0$
  • Lemma: $D$ is a simply connected region, $f(z) \in \text{Hol}(D),\exists F^{\prime}(z)=f(z)$.
  • Lemma: $\Omega$ has $n$ holes, there are $n$ analytic functions without primitives.
  • Theorem: $\{f_n(z) \in \text{Hol}(\Omega)\}$ internally closed $\rightrightarrows f(z) \Rightarrow f(z) \in \text{Hol}(\Omega)$.
