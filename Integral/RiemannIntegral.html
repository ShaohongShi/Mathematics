Riemann Integral

$$\displaystyle{\int_a^b f(x)\mathrm{d}x:=\lim\limits_{\lambda(P) \to 0} \sum\limits_{i=1}^n f(\xi_i)\Delta x_i}$$
  • $\lambda(P): \max\{\Delta x_i=x_i-x_{i-1}, i=1, \cdots,n\}$.
  • $P:a=x_0 < x_1 < \cdots < x_n=b$.
$f$ is bounded on $[a, b]$
  • $\impliedby (f \in \mathcal{R}[a, b])$.
  • $\implies \displaystyle{\lim\limits_{\lambda(P) \to 0}\sum\limits_{i=1}^n M_i \Delta x_i=\overline{\int_a^b}f(x)\mathrm{d}x}$.

    • $(\inf\limits_P {\sum\limits_{i=1}^n M_i \Delta x_i},M_i=\sup\limits_{x \in [x_{i-1},x_i]}\{f(x)\})$.

  • $\implies \displaystyle{\lim\limits_{\lambda(P) \to 0}\sum\limits_{i=1}^n m_i \Delta x_i=\underline{\int_a^b}f(x)\mathrm{d}x}$.

    • $(\sup\limits_P {\sum\limits_{i=1}^n m_i \Delta x_i},m_i=\inf\limits_{x \in [x_{i-1},x_i]}\{f(x)\})$

$f$ is bounded on $[a, b]$, $(f \in \mathcal{R}[a,b])$
  • $\iff \forall \epsilon >0$, $\exists \delta >0$, $\sum\limits_{i=1}^n \omega_i \Delta x_i < \epsilon$, $\forall \lambda(P)<\delta$.
  • $\iff \displaystyle{\overline{\int_a^b}f(x)dx=\underline{\int_a^b}f(x)\mathrm{d}x}$.
  • $\iff f$ is continuous at almost every point.
  • $\impliedby f$ is monotonic.

Vector Space

If $f,g \in \mathcal{R}[a, b]$,
  • $\int\limits_a^b (\alpha f+\beta g)\mathrm{d}x=\alpha \int\limits_a^b f\mathrm{d}x+\beta \int\limits_a^b g\mathrm{d}x$.
  • $f|_{[c,d]} \in \mathcal{R}[a, b], \text{if } [c,d] \subset [a,b]$.
  • $(f \cdot g) \in \mathcal{R}[a, b]$.
  • $\forall \alpha,\beta,\gamma \in [a,b], \int\limits_{\alpha}^{\beta}f\mathrm{d}x+\int\limits_{\beta}^{\gamma}f\mathrm{d}x=\int\limits_{\alpha}^{\gamma}f\mathrm{d}x$.
  • If $g \in C(f([a,b]))$, $g(f) \in \mathcal{R}[a,b]$.

    • $f \in \mathcal{R}[a,b]$, $F(x)=\int_a^x f(t)\mathrm{d}t \implies F(x)\in C[a,b]$.

    If in addition, $f$ is continuous at $x_0 \in [a,b]$, then $F^{\prime}(x_0)=f(x_0)$.

$\phi :[\alpha, \beta] \to [a,b] \in C^1[\alpha, \beta]$, $\phi(\alpha)=a, \phi(\beta)=b$, if $f(x) \in C^1[a,b]$ or $\phi(x)$ is strictly monotonic, $f(x) \in \mathcal{R}[a,b]$ , then $\int _a^b f(x)\mathrm{d}x=\int _{\alpha}^{\beta} f(\phi(x))\phi^{\prime}(x)\mathrm{d}x$.

Inquality

If $f,g \in \mathcal{R}[a, b]$, then
  • $|\int\limits_a^b f\mathrm{d}x| \le \int\limits_a^b |f|\mathrm{d}x$.
  • If $f \le g$ on $[a,b]$, $\int\limits_a^b f\mathrm{d}x \le \int\limits_a^b g\mathrm{d}x$.
  • $\forall \epsilon>0, \exists h \in C[a,b]$, $\int\limits_a^b |f-h|\mathrm{d}x<\epsilon($or $\int\limits_a^b |f-h|^2\mathrm{d}x<\epsilon)$.

    • use step function to simulate.

  • If $\int\limits_a^bf\mathrm{d}x>0$, then $\exists \mu>0, [c,d] \subset [a,b], f>\mu$ on $[c,d]$.
  • If $f>0$ on $[a,b]$, then $\int\limits_a^bf\mathrm{d}x>0$.

Mean-value theorem

  • First: Let $f,g \in \mathcal{R}[a,b]$ and $g$ be sign-preserving on $[a,b]$, then $\int_a^b (f \cdot g)(x)\mathrm{d}x=\mu \int_a^b g(x)\mathrm{d}x$, where $\mu \in [\inf\limits_{x \in [a,b]}f(x), \sup\limits_{x \in [a,b]}f(x)]]$. If, in addition, $f \in C[a,b]$, $\int_a^b (f \cdot g)(x)\mathrm{d}x=f(\xi) \int_a^b g(x)\mathrm{d}x$, where $\xi \in [a,b]$. Let $f,g \in \mathcal{R}[a,b]$ and $g$ be nonnegative and nonincreasing on $[a,b]$, then $\int_a^b (f \cdot g)(x)\mathrm{d}x=g(a) \int_a^{\xi} f(x)\mathrm{d}x$, where $\xi \in [a,b]$.
  • Second Let $f,g \in \mathcal{R}[a,b]$ and $g$ be monotonic on $[a,b]$, then $\int_a^b (f \cdot g)(x)\mathrm{d}x=g(a) \int_a^{\xi} f(x)\mathrm{d}x +g(b) \int_{\xi}^b f(x)\mathrm{d}x$, where $\xi \in [a,b]$.

Fundamental theorem of calculus

  • If $f \in \mathcal{R}[a,b]$ then $\int _a^b f(x)\mathrm{d}x=F(b)-F(a)$, where $F$ is an primitive of $f$ in $[a,b]$.
  • Let $f \in \mathcal{R}[a,b], F(x)=\int _{a}^{x}f(t)\mathrm{d}t, \forall x\in [a,b]$.
    • $F$ is Lipschitz continuous on $[a,b]$.
    • If, in addition, $f \in C[a,b]$, then $f \in C^{1}(a,b)$ with $F^{\prime}(x)=f(x)$.
