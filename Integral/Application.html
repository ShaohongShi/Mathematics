Additive function

  • $I(\alpha,\beta)+I(\beta,\gamma)=I(\alpha,\gamma), \forall \alpha,\beta,\gamma \in[a, b]$, then the function $I(\alpha,\beta)$ is called an additive (oriented) function.
  • Suppose the additive function $I(\alpha,\beta)$ defined on $[a, b]$ and $\exists f \in \mathcal{R}[a, b], \forall a \le \alpha \le \beta \le b$, $$\inf\limits_{x \in [\alpha,\beta]} f(x)(\beta-\alpha) \le I(\alpha, \beta) \le \sup\limits_{x \in [\alpha,\beta]} f(x)(\beta-\alpha)$$ $$\implies I(a,b) = \int_a^b f(x)\mathrm{d}x$$

Guldins theorem

  • The surface area $A$ of a surface of revolution generated by rotating a plane curve $C$ about an axis external to $C$ and on the same plane is equal to the product of the arc length $s$ of $C$ and the distance $d$ traveled by its geometric centroid.
  • The volume $V$ of a solid of revolution generated by rotating a plane figure $F$ about an external axis is equal to the product of the area $A$ of $F$ and the distance $d$ traveled by its geometric centroid.
