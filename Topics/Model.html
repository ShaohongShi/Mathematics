Body of Variable Mass

Consider a rocket moving in a straight line in outer space, far from gravitating bodies.
  • $M(t)$: the mass of the rocket (including fuel) at time t.
  • $V(t)$: its velocity at time $t$.
  • $\omega$: the speed (relative to the rocket) the fuel flows out.
  • $V(t)=V(0)+\omega \ln {\dfrac{M(0)}{M(t)}}$.

Barometric Formula

  • $p(h)$ be the pressure at elevation $h$.
  • $\rho (h)$ be the density of air at elevation $h$.
  • $p(h+\Delta h)-p(h)=-g\rho (\xi)\Delta h, h< \xi < h+\Delta h$.
  • $p=\rho \dfrac{RT}{M}=\lambda (T)\rho$.
  • $p=p_0\mathrm{e}^{-(g/\lambda)h}$

Chain Reactions

We take the substance to have the shape of a sphere of radius $r$, which is not too small
  • $N(t)=N(0)\mathrm{e}^{-\lambda t}$ the number of atoms that have not decayed up to time $t$.
  • $N(t+h)-N(t) \approx \alpha N(t)h-\dfrac{\beta}{r}N(t)h$, where $\alpha, \beta$ depend only on the substance.
  • $N(t)=N(0)\mathrm{e}^{(\alpha-\frac{\beta}{r})t}$.
