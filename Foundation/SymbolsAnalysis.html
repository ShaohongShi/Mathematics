Difference

  • $C^0$: continuous.
  • $C^k$: with $k$-th order continuous derivative.
  • $C^{\infty}$: infinitely derivable.
  • $C^{\omega}$: analytic.

Field Theory

  • Gradient
    • $$\operatorname{grad}f(x_0) = \left(\dfrac{\partial f}{\partial x^1}(x_0),\cdots ,\dfrac{\partial f}{\partial x^m}(x_0)\right) \, .$$ $$D_{\boldsymbol{v}} f({\boldsymbol{x}}_0)=\lim\limits_{t \to 0}\dfrac{f(\boldsymbol{x}_0+\boldsymbol{v}t)-f(\boldsymbol{x}_0)}{t} = \langle \operatorname{grad} f(\boldsymbol{x}_0), \boldsymbol{v} \rangle \, .$$
  • Laplacian
    • $$\Delta f = \nabla^2 f = \nabla \cdot \nabla f = \sum\limits_{i=1}^n \dfrac {\partial^2 f}{\partial x^2_i}, \Delta f(z)=4 \dfrac{\partial^2}{\partial z \partial \bar{z}} \, .$$

Parametrized Curve

  • $z(t):[a, b] \subset \mathbb{R} \to \mathbb{C}^2$.
  • Smooth if $z(t) \in C^1,z^{\prime}(t) \ne 0$.
  • Piecewise-smooth $z \in C^0, z \in C^1$ but finite points.
  • A positively oriented curve is a planar simple closed curve (that is, a curve in the plane whose starting point is also the end point and which has no other self-intersections) such that when traveling on it one always has the curve interior to the left.

Borel measurable

  • $\forall B \in \mathcal{B}(\mathbb{R}),g^{-1}(B) \in \mathcal{B}(\mathbb{R})$
  • Continuous functions and monotone functions are Borel measurable.
