Uniform distribution

  • $f(\boldsymbol{x})=\begin{cases} \dfrac{1}{S} & \boldsymbol{x} \in G \\ \ 0 & \boldsymbol{x} \notin G \end{cases}, G \subset \mathbb{R}^n, G \text{ is bounded with a nonzero measure}$
  • $X \sim U[a,b], EX=\dfrac{a+b}{2}, \operatorname{var}(X)=\dfrac{(b-a)^2}{12}$.
  • Cauchy distribution: $Y=\tan X,X \sim U[-\dfrac{\pi}{2},\dfrac{\pi}{2}], f(y)=\dfrac{1}{\pi(1+y^2)}$.
  • The mathematics expectation of Cauchy distribution does not exist.
  • $F(X) \in C^0,Y=F(X), Y \sim U[0,1]$.
  • $X \sim U[0,1], Y=F^{-1}(X)=\inf\{x \mid F(x)>X\},Y \sim F(x)$.

Gamma Distribution

  • $\Gamma(r,\lambda) \quad f(x)=\dfrac{\lambda^r}{\Gamma(r)}x^{r-1}e^{-\lambda x} (x > 0)$. $r>0$ is the shape parameter, $\lambda>0$ is the scale parameter.
  • $X \sim \Gamma(r_1,\lambda),Y \sim \Gamma(r_2,\lambda)$ are independent, $X+Y,\dfrac{X}{Y}$ are independent.
  • Erlang: $r \in \mathbb{Z}_+, F(W_r=t)=1-\sum\limits_{k=0}^{r-1}\dfrac{(\lambda t)^k e^{\lambda t}}{k!}$, $W_r$ is the time of the $r$th event happens in a Poisson process with $\lambda t$.
  • $\text{Exp}(\lambda))r=0, f(x)=\lambda e^{-\lambda x}, F(x)=1-e^{-\lambda x}, x\ge 0 $

    • $EX=\dfrac{1}{\lambda}$

    $X \text{ is a continuous memoryless r.v.} \Leftrightarrow X \sim \text{Exp}(\lambda)$.

  • Chi -square: \chi_n^2 \overset{d}{=}\Gamma(\dfrac{n}{2},\dfrac{1}{2}) \quad f(x)=\dfrac{1}{2^{\frac{n}{2}}\Gamma\left(\frac{n}{2}\right)}x^{\frac{n}{2}-1}e^{-\frac{x}{2}}(x>0)$.

Normal distribution $N(\mu,\sigma)$

  • $f(x)=\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}\sigma}e^{-\frac{(x-\mu)^2}{2 \sigma^2}} (\sigma >0), EX=\mu, \operatorname{var}(X)=\sigma^2$.
  • $X \overset{i.i.d}{=}Y$ with second order derivable nonzero density function, $X+Y,X-Y$ are independent $\Leftrightarrow X,Y,X+Y,X-Y \sim$ normal distribution.
  • logrithmic normal: $Y=e^X,X \sim (\mu,\sigma^2),f_Y(y)=\dfrac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}\sigma y} e^{-\frac{(\ln y-\mu)^2}{2 \sigma^2}}(y>0)$.

Normal Distribution Multivariable

  • $f(x)=\dfrac{1}{(2\pi)^{\frac{n}{2}}(\det{\Sigma})^{\frac{1}{2}}}e^{-\frac{1}{2}( \boldsymbol{x}-\boldsymbol{\mu})\Sigma^{-1}(\boldsymbol{x}-\boldsymbol{\mu})^T}$.

    • $\Sigma=(\sigma_{ij})$ is a $n$ order positive-definite symmetric matrix.

  • $N(\mu_1,\mu_2,\sigma_1^2,\sigma_2^2,\rho), \Sigma=\begin{bmatrix} \sigma_1^2 & \rho\sigma_1\sigma_2 \\ \rho\sigma_1\sigma_2 & \sigma_2^2 \end{bmatrix}, \mu=(\mu_1,\mu_2), \sigma_1,\sigma_2 >0,|\rho| < 1$.
  • $f(x,y)=\dfrac{\mathrm{exp}\lbrace-\dfrac{1}{2(1-\rho^2)}[\dfrac{(x-\mu_1)^2}{\sigma_1^2} -2\rho\dfrac{(x-\mu_1)(y-\mu_2)}{\sigma_1 \sigma_2}+ \dfrac{(y-\mu_2)^2}{\sigma_2^2} ]\rbrace}{2\pi \sigma_1 \sigma_2 \sqrt{1-\rho^2}}$.
  • $N(\mu_1,\sigma_1^2) \times N(\mu_2+\rho\dfrac{\sigma_2}{\sigma_1}(x-\mu_1),\sigma_2^2(1-\rho^2))$, $N(\mu_2,\sigma_2^2) \times N(\mu_1+\rho\dfrac{\sigma_1}{\sigma_2}(y-\mu_2),\sigma_1^2(1-\rho^2))$.
  • $X \overset{i.i.d.}{=}Y \sim N(\mu,\sigma^2),\sqrt{X^2+Y^2} \sim \text{Rayleigh } R(r)=re^{-\frac{r^2}{2}}, r \ge 0$.
  • X_1,\cdots,X_n \sim N(0,1)$ are independent, $X=X_1^2+\cdots+X_n^2 \sim \chi_n^2$.
