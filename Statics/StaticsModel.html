MLE

The method of maximum likelihood is based on the likelihood function, $\mathcal {L}(\theta ;x)$. We are given a statistical model, i.e. a family of distributions $\{f(\cdot \,;\theta )\mid \theta \in \Theta \}$, where $\theta$ denotes the (possibly multi-dimensional) parameter for the model. The method of maximum likelihood finds the values of the model parameter, $\theta$, that maximize the likelihood function $\mathcal {L}(\theta ;x)$. $\hat{\Theta}=\{ \mathrm{argmax}_{\theta \in \Theta} \mathcal{L}(\theta;x)\}$ if a maximum exists.

Statistical hypothesis test

Commonly, two statistical data sets are compared, or a data set obtained by sampling is compared against a synthetic data set from an idealized model. A hypothesis is proposed for the statistical relationship between the two data sets, and this is compared as an alternative to an idealized null hypothesis that proposes no relationship between two data sets.
