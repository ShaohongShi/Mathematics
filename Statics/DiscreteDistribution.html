Poisson $\mathcal{P}(\lambda)$

  • Stationary: the distribution of each interval only depends on the length.
  • $P_k(t)=P(k \text{ events in time }t),\sum\limits_{k=0}^{\infty}P_k(t)=1, \lim\limits_{t \to 0}\sum\limits_{k=2}^{\infty}P_k(t)=o(t)$.
  • The number of points in disjoint intervals are independent.
  • $f(k;\lambda)={\dfrac {\lambda ^{k}e^{-\lambda }}{k!}}, \lambda >0, k=0,1, \cdots$.
  • $k$ is the events observed and $\lambda$ the mean of events in interval $t_0$.
  • $EX=\operatorname{var}(X)=\lambda$.
  • $X_1 \sim \mathcal{P} (\lambda_1), X_2 \sim \mathcal{P} (\lambda_2), X_1+X_2 \sim \mathcal{P} (\lambda_1+\lambda_2)$.

Binomial $B(n,p)$

  • $B(n,p): b(k;n,p)=\dbinom{n}{k}p^{k}(1-p)^{n-k}$. When $k=1$, it is Bernoulli distribution.
  • $k$ successes in a sequence of $n$ independent Bernoulli experiments $(1,p)$.
  • $EX=np, \operatorname{var}(X)=npq$.
  • $b(k;n,p_n) \to \dfrac {\lambda ^{k}e^{-\lambda }}{k!}, np_n \to \lambda, n \to \infty$.
  • Maximum: $k=\begin{cases}[(n+1)p] & \text{ if } (n+1)p \notin \mathbb{Z} \\ (n+1)p, (n+1)p-1 & \text{ if } (n+1)p \in \mathbb{Z} \end{cases}$.
  • $X_1 \sim B(n_1,p),X_2 \sim B(n_2,p),X_1+X_2 \sim B(n_1+n_2,p)$.

Hypergeometric $H(N,M,n)$

  • $h_k={\dfrac {{\binom {M}{k}}{\binom {N-M}{n-k}}}{\binom {N}{n}}}, 0 \le k \le n \le N, k\le M$.
  • $N$ is the population size, $M$ is the number of success, $n$ is the number of draws.
  • $EX=\dfrac{nM}{N}$.
  • $h_k\approx\dbinom{n}{k}(\dfrac{M}{N})^k (1-\dfrac{M}{N})^{n-k}, M >> k, N-M >> n-k$.

Geometric

  • $g(k,p)=(1-p)^{k-1}p, k=1,2, \cdots$.
  • $k$ tries before first success in Bernoulli trials with the probability of success $p$.
  • $EX=\dfrac{1}{p}$
  • Memoryless: $X \in \mathbb{Z}^+, P(X=\eta+k|X>\eta)=P(X=k) \Leftrightarrow X \sim g(k,p)$.

Other

  • Pascal: $P(k;r,p)=\dbinom{k-1}{r-1}p^r (1-p)^{k-r}$. The probability of $k$ trials needed to see $r$ successes in a Bernoulli process.
  • Negative binomial: $Nb(l;r,p)=\dbinom{-r}{l}p^r(-q)^l$. The probablity of observing $l$ failures before $r$ success.
  • Banach: $\dbinom{2N-r}{N}\left(\dfrac{1}{2}\right)^{2N-r}$. Two matchboxes originally contained $N$ matches each, the probability that there are exactly $r$ matches in the other box when the first time that the box picked is empty.
