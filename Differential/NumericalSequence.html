Approximation

$x$ is the exact value of a quantity, $\tilde{x}$ a known approximation to the quantity,
  • Absolute $\Delta (\tilde{x}) := |x-\tilde{x}|$, relative $\delta (\tilde {x}):=\dfrac{\Delta (\tilde{x})}{|\tilde{x}|}$.
  • $\Delta (\tilde{x}+\tilde{y}) \le \Delta(\tilde{x})+\Delta(\tilde{y}), \delta (\tilde{x}+\tilde{y}) \le \dfrac{\Delta(\tilde{x})+\Delta(\tilde{y})}{|\tilde{x}+\tilde{y}|}$
  • $\Delta (\tilde{x} \cdot \tilde{y}) \le |\tilde{x}|\Delta(\tilde{y})+|\tilde{y}|\Delta(\tilde{x})$
  • $\delta (\tilde{x} \cdot \tilde{y}) \le \delta(\tilde{x})+\delta(\tilde{y})+\delta(\tilde{x}) \cdot \delta(\tilde{y})$
  • $\Delta\left(\dfrac{\tilde{x}}{\tilde{y}}\right) \le \dfrac{|\tilde x| \Delta(\tilde y)+|\tilde y| \Delta(\tilde x)}{\tilde{y}^2(1-\delta(\tilde y))}, \delta\left(\dfrac{\tilde x}{\tilde y} \right) \le \dfrac{\delta(\tilde x)+\delta(\tilde y)}{1-\delta(\tilde y)}$

Prosperity

  • Limits of sequences behave well with four arithmetic operations.
  • (Squeeze) If $x_n \le z_n \le y_n, \forall n>N,$ and $\lim\limits_{n\to \infty }x_n=\lim\limits_{n\to \infty }y_n=L$, then $\lim\limits_{n \to \infty}z_n=L.$
  • (Cauchy) If $\lim\limits_{n \to \infty}{x_n}=x$, $\lim\limits_{n \to \infty}{y_n}=y$,

    • $\lim\limits_{n \to \infty} \dfrac{1}{n}\sum\limits_{i=0}^n x_i = x, \lim\limits_{n \to \infty}.

    \dfrac{1}{n}\sum\limits_{i=0}^n x_iy_{n-i} = xy$.

    In addtional, $x_n>0$, $\lim\limits_{n \to \infty}(\prod\limits_{i=1}^{n}x_i)^{\frac{1}{n}}=x$.

  • (Stolz) $\lim\limits_{n \to \infty}\dfrac{a_{n+1}-a_n}{b_{n+1}-b_n} = l \Rightarrow \lim\limits_{n \to \infty}\dfrac{a_n}{b_n}=l$ if

    • $\{b_n\}$ is strictly monotone and divergent sequence.

    $\{a_n\}$ and $\{b_n\}$ be infinitesimal, $\{b_n\}$ is strictly monotone.

Upper and Lower limit

$\mathop{\underline{\lim}}\limits_{n \to \infty}x_k := \lim\limits_{n \to \infty}\inf\limits_{k \ge n}x_k, \mathop{\overline{\lim}}\limits_{n \to \infty}x_k := \lim\limits_{n \to \infty}\sup\limits_{k \ge n}x_k$.
  • Partial limit is the limit of a converging subsequence, the inferior and superior limits of a bounded sequence are respectively the smallest and largest.
  • A sequence has a limit or tends to negative or positive infinity if and only if its inferior and superior limits are the same.
For bounded sequences $\{x_n\}, \{y_n\}, x_n < y_n$
  • $\mathop{\underline{\lim}}\limits_{n \to \infty} (-x_n) = -\mathop{\overline{\lim}}\limits_{n \to \infty}x_n$, $\mathop{\overline{\lim}}\limits_{n \to \infty} (-x_n) = -\mathop{\underline{\lim}}\limits_{n \to \infty}x_n$
  • $\mathop{\underline{\lim}}\limits_{n \to \infty}x_n \le \mathop{\underline{\lim}}\limits_{n \to \infty}y_n$, $\mathop{\overline{\lim}}\limits_{n \to \infty}x_n \le \mathop{\overline{\lim}}\limits_{n \to \infty}y_n$.
  • $\mathop{\underline{\lim}}\limits_{n \to \infty}x_n+\mathop{\underline{\lim}}\limits_{n \to \infty}y_n \le \mathop{\underline{\lim}}\limits_{n \to \infty}(x_n+y_n) \le \mathop{\underline{\lim}}\limits_{n \to \infty}x_n+\mathop{\overline{\lim}}\limits_{n \to \infty}y_n \le \mathop{\overline{\lim}}\limits_{n \to \infty}(x_n+y_n) \le \mathop{\overline{\lim}}\limits_{n \to \infty}x_n+\mathop{\overline{\lim}}\limits_{n \to \infty}y_n$.
  • If $x_n, y_n>0, \mathop{\underline{\lim}}\limits_{n \to \infty}x_n \cdot \mathop{\underline{\lim}}\limits_{n \to \infty}y_n \le \mathop{\underline{\lim}}\limits_{n \to \infty}(x_n \cdot y_n) \le \mathop{\underline{\lim}}\limits_{n \to \infty}x_n \cdot \mathop{\overline{\lim}}\limits_{n \to \infty}y_n \le \mathop{\overline{\lim}}\limits_{n \to \infty}(x_n \cdot y_n) \le \mathop{\overline{\lim}}\limits_{n \to \infty}x_n \cdot \mathop{\overline{\lim}}\limits_{n \to \infty}y_n$
