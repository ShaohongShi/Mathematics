Index Numerical Series Power Series

Definition

  • Ascoli: $\{f_n(x) \in \mathbb{R},x \in [a,b]\}_{n \in \mathbb{N}}$ is uniformly bounded and equicontinuous, then there exists a subsequence that converges uniformly.
  • Power series: $f(z) = \sum\limits_{n=0}^\infty a_n (z-z_0)^n, z_0 \in \mathbb{C}$.
  • Radius of convergence: $r:=\sup {\{|z-z_0| \big| f(z) \text{ converges }\}}=\dfrac{1}{\mathop{\overline{\lim}}\limits_{n \to \infty}\sqrt[n]{a_n}}$
  • Abel: $f(z)$ converges at $z^* \Rightarrow f(z)$ converges absolutely at $\forall |z-z_0|<|z^*- z_0|$.
  • Analytic: $f^{\prime}(z)=\sum\limits_{n=1}^{\infty}na_n(z-z_0)^n$ with the same radius of convergence.

Maclaurin Formula

  • $\mathrm{e}^x=\sum\limits_{i=0}^n\dfrac{x^i}{i!}+o\left(x^n\right)$.
  • $\sin x=\sum\limits_{i=1}^n {(-1)}^{i-1}\dfrac{x^{2i-1}}{(2i-1)!}+o(x^{2n})$.
  • $\cos x=\sum\limits_{i=0}^n{(-1)}^i\dfrac{x^{2i}}{(2i)!}+o(x^{2n})$.
  • $\arcsin x=\sum\limits_{i=0}^n \dfrac{{[(2i-1)!!]}^2 x^{2i+1}}{(2i+1)!}+o(x^n)$.
  • $\ln{(1+x)}=\sum\limits_{i=1}^n\dfrac{{(-1)}^{i-1}x^i}{i}+o(x^n)$.
