Index Numerical Series Power Series

Definition

  • Infinite series: $\sum\limits_{n=1}^{\infty}a_n:=\lim\limits_{n \to \infty } \sum\limits_{k=1}^na_k$.
  • Absolutely convergent: \sum\limits_{n=1}^{\infty}|a_n|$ converges.
  • Cauchy: $\sum\limits_{n=1}^{\infty}a_n$ converges if $\forall \varepsilon > 0,\exists N \in \mathbb{N}, \forall m>n>N, \sum\limits_{i=n}^{m}a_n< \varepsilon$.
  • Comparison: $\exists N, \forall n > N, 0 < a_n < b_n$,

    • $\sum\limits_{n=1}^{\infty}b_n$ converges $\Rightarrow \sum\limits_{n=1}^{\infty}a_n$ converges.

    $\sum\limits_{n=1}^{\infty}a_n$ diverges $\Rightarrow \sum\limits_{n=1}^{\infty}b_n$ diverges.

  • $\alpha=\begin{cases} \limsup\limits_{n \to \infty}\sqrt[n]{|a_n|}(Cauchy) \\ \lim\limits_{n \to \infty}\left| \dfrac{a_{n+1}}{a_n}\right|(d'Alembert)\end{cases}$,then $\sum\limits_{n \to \infty} a_n $ absolutely converges when $\alpha >1$ and diverges when $\alpha < 1$
  • Integral: $f(x)>0, \downarrow,\mathcal{R}$ on each closed interval of $[1,+\infty)$, then $\sum\limits_n^{+\infty}f(n)$ and $\displaystyle{\int_1^{+\infty}f(x)\mathrm{d}x}$ either both converge or both diverge.
