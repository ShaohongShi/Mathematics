Exact Equation

Assume the functions we discuss are continuous.
  • Exact equation: $P(x,y){\rm d}x+Q(x,y){\rm d}y = {\rm d}\Phi(x)$.

    • If $\dfrac{\partial P}{\partial y},\dfrac{\partial Q}{\partial x} \in C^0$, then $\exists \Phi(x) \Leftrightarrow \dfrac{\partial P}{\partial y} \equiv \dfrac{\partial Q}{\partial x}$.

  • Variable separation: $P(x)=X_1(x)Y_1(x), Q(x)=X_2(x)Y_2(x)$.
  • Homogeneous factor: $y=z^{\alpha}\Rightarrow \mu=\dfrac{1}{xP+yQ}$ or $z=ux$.
  • Linear: $\dfrac{{\rm d}y}{{\rm d}x}+p(x)y=q(x),\displaystyle{y={\rm e}^{-\int p(x){\rm d}x}(C+\int q(x) {\rm e}^{\int p(x) {\rm d}x}\mathrm{d}x)}$.
  • Bernoulli: $\dfrac{{\rm d}y}{{\rm d}x}+p(x)y = q(x)y^n,z=y^{1-n}$.
  • $\mu P{\rm d}x+\mu Q{\rm d}y={\rm d}F,\begin{cases}G(x)=\frac{1}{Q}\left(\frac{\partial P}{\partial y}-\frac{\partial Q}{\partial x}\right) \\ G(y)=\frac{1}{P}\left(\frac{\partial Q}{\partial x}-\frac{\partial P}{\partial y}\right)\end{cases}, \mu(\cdot)={\rm e}^{\int G(\cdot){\rm d}\cdot}$.
  • $\mu_1P{\rm d}x+\mu_1 Q{\rm d}y={\rm d}F_1 \Leftrightarrow \exists g(\cdot) \ne 0 \text{ is devirable},\mu_1=\mu g(F)$.

Gronwall Inequality

    Let $I$ denote $[a,\infty),[a, b],[a, b),a < b, \beta,u \in C^0(I)$.
  • In $I^0, u$ is differentiable, $u'(t)\leq \beta (t)u(t)$, then $u(t)\leq u(a){\rm e}^{\int _{a}^{t}\beta (s)\,{\mathrm {d}}s}$, $\forall t \in I$.
  • The negative part of $\alpha$ is integrable on every closed and bounded subinterval of $I$.

    2. If, in addition, the function $\alpha$ is non-decreasing, then $u(t)\leq \alpha (t){\rm e}^{\int _{a}^{t}\beta (s)\,{\mathrm {d}}s}, t\in I$.
